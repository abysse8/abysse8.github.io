My long-term goal is to build a DNA-based artificial intelligence by reverse-engineering the neuron — not its abstract weights-and-bits caricature, but its actual molecular dynamics — and recreating that logic in a chemical substrate. The idea is that neural computation can be re-expressed in terms of concentrations, conformations, reaction propensities, and feedback loops rather than digital operations. The path runs from interpreting the neuron's physics (ion channels, calcium, gene regulation), to modeling it computationally, to translating it into DNA strand-displacement and CRISPR-based circuits. What's unresolved is whether molecular substrates can actually scale to anything resembling intelligence, or even faithfully mimic a single neuron's learning behavior.
