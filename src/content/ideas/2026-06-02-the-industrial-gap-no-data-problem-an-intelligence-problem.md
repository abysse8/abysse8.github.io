My angle is that industrial infrastructure already drowns in data — logs, SNMP, metrics, alarms, history — so the real problem isn't collecting it, it's that current systems are reactive, static, and built on hand-coded thresholds. Real intelligence starts when you detect slow drifts, correlations, and precursor signatures that raise no individual alarm but together predict failure. On a mesh AP, rising latency, dropping RSSI, frequent neighbor changes, and higher retransmission rates jointly signal degradation before anything breaks. My edge is sitting at the intersection few occupy: embedded + network + signal + AI — AI people don't grasp the physical field, and infra people can't exploit the data.
