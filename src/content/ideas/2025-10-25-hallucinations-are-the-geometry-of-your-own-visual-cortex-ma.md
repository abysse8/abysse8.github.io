I got curious about why psychedelic visuals are so stereotyped across people — the same spirals, tunnels, grids, and kaleidoscopes keep showing up. The idea that clicked is that these aren't random: the visual cortex (V1) is a geometrically organized grid, and when drugs disinhibit it, it starts perceiving its own internal models as if they were sensory input — a visual feedback loop. The recurring shapes are the 'entoptic forms,' the literal signature of the cortex running without stable input. What's striking is that these patterns were modeled mathematically (Cowan & Ermentrout) and real hallucinations follow those cortical wave equations. So what you 'see' is the math of your own brain exciting itself.
