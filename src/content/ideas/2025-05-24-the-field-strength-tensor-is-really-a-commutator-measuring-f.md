I noticed that the way the relativistic electromagnetic field gets written down looks structurally like a commutator, and I wanted to understand whether that resemblance is meaningful. It turns out it's not a coincidence: the antisymmetric field strength tensor $F_{\mu\nu}=\partial_\mu A_\nu-\partial_\nu A_\mu$ generalizes in non-Abelian gauge theory to literally become the commutator of covariant derivatives, $F_{\mu\nu}=[D_\mu,D_\nu]$. In that light the field strength is a measure of how much the space 'fails to commute' — the same intuition behind curvature and force. What's still open for me is walking the full path from special relativity through covariant electrodynamics into gauge theory and QFT to feel why this must be so.
