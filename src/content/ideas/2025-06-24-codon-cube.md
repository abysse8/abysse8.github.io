The 64 codons are almost always drawn as a flat wheel or table, which hides the
structure that matters. Arrange them instead as a $4 \times 4 \times 4$ lattice —
one axis per nucleotide position — and the genetic code stops being a lookup and
starts being a *geometry*. Synonymous codons cluster; the wobble position becomes
a literal spatial axis you can collapse. The open question: if translation is a
walk through this cube, does the third-position degeneracy encode something like
an error-correcting margin — redundancy placed exactly where the physical read is
least reliable?
