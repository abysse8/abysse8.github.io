My question was: what is an antidepressant actually doing, especially for someone who regulates themselves through analysis, prediction, and internal control? The reframe that landed is that these drugs don't simply 'boost serotonin' — they loosen the rigid, self-reinforcing loops the brain has built, forcing a period of instability before stability. For an analytical person who navigates by precise internal signals, this destabilization feels like getting worse: mismatched prediction errors, reduced cognitive grip, emotional noise. But that discomfort is the rewiring phase — a controlled demolition before the new structure is built. What's unresolved is how to distinguish normal destabilization from a genuinely bad side effect.
