I run — or am joining the orbit of — a small cable-harness company whose only real client is a single systems integrator, doing wire harnesses and interface boards for buses and public works. My worry is we can't find work because big integrators own all the contracts. The reframe I'm chasing: the real problem isn't lack of skill, it's that pure assembly labor is interchangeable and low-margin. If we climb the value chain — distributed sensors, CAN bus integration, Ethernet diagnostics, modular I/O, predictive maintenance — we stop selling hours and start selling systems that customers can't easily replace. What's unresolved is whether the company actually wants that transition, and where I personally fit into driving it.
