I'm chasing a structural parallel: the same math that says an analytic function's real and imaginary parts are harmonic ($\nabla^2 u = 0$) shows up everywhere as a stationarity condition — least action, electrostatic equilibrium, QED field equations. My hunch is that the analyticity condition $\partial f/\partial\bar z = 0$ isn't just a technical smoothness requirement but a kind of coherence/equilibrium condition, and that when it's *violated* you get the non-equilibrium dynamics that neurons actually exhibit when they fire. Steady ion gradients and resting membrane potentials would be the 'harmonic' regime; spikes and thresholds would be the poles and residues. What's unresolved is whether this is a genuine physical mapping or just a suggestive analogy across domains that happen to share a differential operator.
