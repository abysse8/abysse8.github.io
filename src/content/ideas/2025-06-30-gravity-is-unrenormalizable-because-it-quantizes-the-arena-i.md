I couldn't see why gravity should be so much harder to quantize than the other forces — it has a field, a Lagrangian, and a particle (the graviton), just like the rest. The answer that clicked: unlike photons that couple to charge, gravitons couple to energy, and gravitons themselves carry energy, so they couple to each other in an infinite feedback loop that produces new uncontrollable infinities at every loop order. Deeper still, gravity isn't a field living on spacetime — it *is* spacetime, so quantizing it means quantizing the stage everything else stands on. String theory sidesteps this by smearing interactions over a 1D string instead of a 0D point, which tames the singularities.
