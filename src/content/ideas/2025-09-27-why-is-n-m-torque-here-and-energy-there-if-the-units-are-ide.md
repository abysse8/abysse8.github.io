Newton-meters show up as the unit for both torque and work/energy, and they carry the same dimensions (kg·m²/s²) — so why do we treat them as different physical quantities? My hunch, sharpened here, is that the distinction lives entirely in geometry and context rather than in units: in work, force and displacement lie along the same line and produce energy transfer; in torque, they're perpendicular and produce a rotational tendency. That's why '1 N·m of torque is not 1 J of energy' even when the numbers match. The unit alone doesn't tell you which quantity you mean — the directionality does.
