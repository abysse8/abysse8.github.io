I'm exploring whether a neuron uses its ATP consumption rate as an aggregated informational signal about work done — a kind of internal accounting — rather than treating ATP purely as an energy constraint. The hunch is that if the cell integrates ATP usage across space and time, that signal could drive transcription, membrane transport remodeling, and synaptic plasticity. There's no literal ledger in one organelle, but AMPK/mTOR sensing of the AMP:ATP ratio, activity-dependent mitochondrial repositioning, and local ATP gradients seen with FRET sensors all point to functional integration. What's unresolved is whether this genuinely functions as a computational variable tied to memory encoding and structural plasticity, or is just downstream homeostasis.
