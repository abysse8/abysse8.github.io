I kept tripping on why quantum energy-level interactions should be *multiplicative* — to me superposition felt immiscible, like overlaid waves that interfere but never blend. The resolution: superposition is additive because it's just coexisting possibilities, but multiplication enters when an operator *acts*, composing transitions step by step. So the matrix product $\sum_k X_{mk}X_{kn}$ isn't multiplying quantities of energy — it's summing amplitudes over all intermediate paths from one state to another. That's exactly Heisenberg's move: build the theory from observable transitions, and their sequential composition forces matrix multiplication. Multiplication is about sequencing, not amount.
