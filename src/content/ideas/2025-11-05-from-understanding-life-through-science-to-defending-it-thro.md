I wanted to frame a personal-narrative statement for a public-service interview: a man who went to university chasing one specific question — how a living being turns a signal into perception — built machines and circuits to answer it, but never found what he was really looking for. What he found instead, outside the lab, was fear, violence, and human suffering that no electrical signal could explain. So the arc is about converting scientific rigor, precision, and discipline into something protective and concrete. The core line I want to land: I once tried to understand life through science; now I want to defend it through action.
