I've noticed my instinct is to change five things at once when something's broken — it feels like momentum, but it destroys attribution: afterward I have no idea what actually fixed it. My hunch is this is the same failure across plant growing, embedded debugging, distributed systems, and ML tuning. The deeper point is that the mind that's good at design — building a graph of interconnected causes instantly — is exactly the wrong mind for debugging, which requires aggressively collapsing dimensions to isolate one variable. Engineering maturity is the ability to tolerate uncertainty long enough to isolate. What's open is how to deliberately switch modes when my default is fast systems-coupling.
