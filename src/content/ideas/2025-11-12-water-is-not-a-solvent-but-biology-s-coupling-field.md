My hunch is that the interaction energy in water doesn't converge — the pairwise potential decays too slowly (like $r^{-3}$) for the total integrated energy to settle. If that's right, water never becomes truly uncorrelated, which means polymers dissolved in it are never in a purely local environment. Their conformations, folding, and collective motions would all be entangled with water's long-range fluctuating order. So DNA, proteins, and cytoskeletal polymers aren't isolated chains in neutral medium — they're coupled to a thermodynamically active field. What's unresolved is whether the correlation length is genuinely divergent or just large (~0.3–1 nm) relative to molecular scales.
