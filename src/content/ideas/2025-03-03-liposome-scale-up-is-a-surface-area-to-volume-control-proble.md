I think the core difficulty in scaling up liposome production is fundamentally a surface-area-to-volume problem. At small scale every liposome sees a nearly uniform microenvironment, but as the reactor grows the lower surface-area-to-volume ratio means temperature, mixing, and solvent evaporation stop being uniform. Dense packing then makes local conditions inconsistent, so some liposomes self-assemble under suboptimal conditions and you get variation in size and encapsulation efficiency. In effect, scale-up introduces mass and heat transfer limitations that just aren't pronounced in the lab. What's still open is whether framing it this way points to a concrete engineering fix.
