I wanted to understand how the high-dimensional vector principles I'd been exploring (hyperdimensional computing, quasi-orthogonality, superposition) actually show up inside large language models. The mapping turns out to be direct: embeddings are the HD vectors, semantic separation is the near-orthogonality, hidden states are superpositions of active features, and attention's dot product is exactly the correlation-based memory retrieval we'd been testing. The key difference is that LLM vectors are learned and optimized rather than random — but the underlying geometry is the same. What still fascinates me is how reasoning emerges as a trajectory through this latent space rather than from explicit symbolic rules.
