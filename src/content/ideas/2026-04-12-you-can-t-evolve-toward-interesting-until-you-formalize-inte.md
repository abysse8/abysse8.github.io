I want to fine-tune my artificial-life simulation with an evolutionary algorithm, but that requires a fitness function — and in my world the only honest fitness is 'produces patterns interesting to me.' The problem is that 'interesting' isn't a physical quantity; it's perceptual and observer-dependent, so any metric I pick risks trivial collapse, noise, or reward-hacking. My hunch is that what I actually respond to is 'order and change at the same time,' which suggests candidate proxies like entropy balance, compression complexity, or cluster persistence. Choosing the metric isn't a detail — it literally defines what counts as life-like in my universe. Open question: whether a multi-objective or human-in-the-loop signal beats trying to formalize aesthetics directly.
