I imagined a kitchen tool you could just submerge in boiling water that would seal shut and hold high pressure inside — a neat gadget for Tyndallization/sterilization. My hunch was that something like this must already be on the market. But the physics kills it: if the outside is at 1 atm, the inside can't climb much past 100°C without becoming an explosion risk, so you genuinely need a purpose-built pressure vessel with gauges, valves, and locking lugs. So the real choice is classic Tyndallization with jars and a pot, or a proper autoclave.
