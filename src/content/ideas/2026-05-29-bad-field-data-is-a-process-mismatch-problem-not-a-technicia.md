When technicians keep filling out the field-service software's fields wrong, the instinct is to demand they 'lock down' and be more careful — but I think that's the wrong approach. The data model was built around what the office wants to receive, not around what a technician naturally observes standing inside a vehicle with tools in hand. Since every vehicle is unique, forcing exact field entry is unnatural and actually complicates things. The better move is to capture reality once (video, photos, voice notes, layouts) and let the system reuse that structured data everywhere, rather than blaming operators for a process fault. The real goal isn't 'AI writes the A2' — it's 'technician captures reality once, system reuses it.'
