I want to formalize the felt connection between wavelets and energy-based learning. My hunch is that both are really about the same thing: wavelets conserve a signal's energy while redistributing it across scales, and learning systems minimize energy over a loss landscape. The bridge is information — wavelet detail coefficients act like prediction errors ('what remains unexplained after accounting for coarse structure'), so a wavelet decomposition is a scale-by-scale residual model, exactly what an intelligent system does. If good representations concentrate energy and information into few degrees of freedom, then intelligence is the discovery of coordinate systems in which reality becomes sparse, predictable, and compressible. What's unresolved is whether this is a rigorous formalization or a suggestive analogy.
